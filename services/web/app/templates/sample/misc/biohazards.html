Samples Index Biohazard Information

This is considered to be the lowest risk of the four levels. It is suitable for work with the presence of low-risk microbes and agents. These will carry little to no threat of infection to healthy adults.

Level One

Procedures and Practices

  1. You must wash your hands before and after handling materials, and before leaving the laboratory.
  2. Mouth pipetting is strictly prohibited.
  3. Eating, drinking, smoking, the application of contact lenses, storing food are not permitted in areas in which this material is found. Refrigerators and freezers ued to store biospecimens should have no other material stored within.
  4. Avoid sharps, such a Pasteur pipettes, needles and scalpels.
  5. Minimise the creation of aerosols and splashes.
  6. Decontaminate work surfaces after work using microbial agents.

Safety Equipment

  1. Laboratory coats are strongly recommended.
  2. You must use protective eyewear when undertaking procedures that may create aerosols and splashes of potentially hazardous materials.
  3. Gloves should be worn when handling materials. Gloves should be changed if contaminated or damaged. Gloves should not be reused, and alternatives to latex should be available.

Facilities

  1. Sink.
  2. Acids-bases-heat-and water resistant laboratory benches should be used.
